Introduction to Measurement

Measurement is the process of finding the size, length, mass, or time of something using numbers and standard units. It helps us compare objects and describe the world accurately in science and daily life.

1.1 Physical Quantities & Units ‧₊˚

Physical Quantity Unit Symbol
Length Meter (m)
Mass Kilogram (kg)
Time Second (s)
Temperature Degree Celsius (°C)

These are standard units used in the International System of Units (SI).

1.2 Standard Form & Prefix ‧₊˚

Standard form is used to write very large or very small numbers.

It is written as: a × 10n

  • 1 ≤ a < 10
  • n is an integer

Example:

  • 3,000 = 3 × 10³
  • 0.00045 = 4.5 × 10⁻⁴
Prefix Symbol Value
Kilo k 10³
Hecto h 10²
Deca da 10¹
Deci d 10⁻¹
Centi c 10⁻²
Milli m 10⁻³

Examples:

  • 1 km = 1,000 m
  • 1 cm = 0.01 m
  • 1 kg = 1,000 g
  • 1 mg = 0.001 g

1.3 Convert from one unit to another ‧₊˚

To convert units:

  • Multiply when converting to smaller units
  • Divide when converting to larger units

Example 1 (Length)

  • 1 km = 1000 m
  • 5 km = 5 × 1000 = 5000 m

Example 2 (Mass)

  • 1 kg = 1000 g
  • 3000 g = 3000 ÷ 1000 = 3 kg

Example 3 (Time)

  • 1 minute = 60 seconds
  • 2 minutes = 2 × 60 = 120 seconds

1.4 Measuring using tools ‧₊˚

different measuring tools are used to measure different physical quantities accurately. Each tool has its own function and way of reading the measurement.

Trible Beam Balance

Function: A triple beam balance is used to measure mass of an object (unit Gram/g)

Parts:

  • Three beams (each beam measures different values)
  • Sliding riders (small weights that move)
  • Pan (place the object here)
  • Pointer (shows if it is balanced)

How to Read It:

  1. Place the object on the pan
  2. Move the largest rider first until the pointer drops.
  3. Move it back one step.
  4. Repeat with the middle rider.
  5. Finally, adjust the smallest rider carefully.
  6. Add all three values together.

When the pointer is exactly at zero, the mass is correct.

Example:

100 g + 20 g + 3.5 g = 123.5 g

Graduated Cylinder

Function: Used to measure the volume of liquids. (Unit: Milliliter/ mL or cubic centimeter/cm³ )

How to Read It:

  1. Place the cylinder on a flat surface.
  2. Look at the liquid at eye level.
  3. Read the measurement at the bottom of the curve (called the meniscus).

Always read the bottom of the meniscus, not the top. Graduated cylinders are more accurate than beakers.

Thermometer

Function: Used to measure temperature. (Unit:Degree Celsius/°C)

Types:

  • Clinical thermometer → measures body temperature
  • Laboratory thermometer → used in experiments

How to Read It:

  1. Place the thermometer in the substance.
  2. Wait until the liquid inside stops moving.
  3. Read the number where the liquid line stops.

Example:

If the liquid stops at 25, the temperature is 25°C.

Function: Used to measure temperature. (Unit:Degree Celsius/°C)
